01
Introduction

Affordable ICT for All Batswana

In pursuit of its mandate of ensuring affordability of communications services and in line with the ideals of the Botswana Economic Transformation Programme (BETP), BOCRA conducted a comprehensive costing and pricing study aimed at determining the actual costs of delivering various communications services.

The study, conducted in consultation with industry stakeholders, covered both wholesale and retail services across Mascom Wireless Botswana, Orange Botswana, and Botswana Telecommunications Corporation (BTC).

Following the study, BOCRA directed all operators to implement revised tariffs aligned to actual cost structures — with significant reductions in mobile data prices underscoring the increasing importance of data in Botswana's digital transformation.

BOCRA's Mandate

"BOCRA remains committed to ensuring that Batswana enjoy accessible, reliable, and affordable communication services, recognising ICT as a key enabler of socio-economic development."

BoFiNet Broadband

BoFiNet Wholesale Broadband Reductions

BOCRA also engaged Botswana Fibre Networks (BoFiNet) to revise its wholesale broadband offerings, approved in October 2025. Prices for National Leased Lines and IP Transit have been reduced by 10%–93% across various capacity offerings.

Retail Services

Approved Prepaid Tariffs

BOCRA directed MNOs to submit revised retail tariffs by 15 August 2025. Mascom Wireless and Orange Botswana received approval. BTC did not adjust its tariffs. Approved tariffs serve as price ceilings for prepaid voice, SMS, and data.

Key Finding: Data Leads the Way
While minor adjustments were made to voice tariffs, significant reductions were observed in mobile data prices — underscoring data's increasing importance in Botswana's digital transformation journey.

Conclusion

Impact & Outlook

The reduction in wholesale and retail services is expected to enhance market competitiveness and consumer welfare through lower prices across Botswana.

Lower Prices
Wholesale and retail reductions drive down the cost of communications for all consumers and businesses, making connectivity more accessible across Botswana.
Market Competition
Reduced termination rates level the playing field between operators, spurring innovation, better service offerings, and improved quality for subscribers.
Digital Inclusion
Affordable data empowers rural communities and individuals without traditional banking access to participate fully in Botswana's digital economy.
