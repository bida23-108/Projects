Executive Intelligence

Strategic Insights & Key Observations

Q3 → Q4 FY2025/26
🗠
Growth Leader
Mascom Mobile Money surges +24.7% — standout performer of the quarter
Mascom's mobile money subscriptions grew from 367,214 to 457,738, the highest growth rate across all market segments. Signals aggressive platform expansion and customer acquisition success.
🛰
Emerging Disruptor
Starlink fixed broadband accelerates +15.0%, reshaping rural connectivity
Starlink grew from 17,352 to 19,958 subscribers, the fastest growth in fixed broadband. LEO satellite is demonstrating strong demand in underserved areas, outpacing all terrestrial operators.
📾
Broadcasting
Gabz FM SAR recovers to 96% after equipment replacement drives reliability
Gabz FM reversed its Q3 decline (93.04% → 96.00%) through strategic transmitter and antenna replacements, demonstrating effective infrastructure investment responding to regulatory oversight.
⌯⌲
Structural Decline
Ordinary mail falls -12.8% QoQ; digital substitution accelerating
Ordinary mail declined from 640,960 to 559,126 items — continuing a sustained structural downtrend driven by digitalisation. Post-festive normalisation amplified the effect this quarter.
⎚
Market Shift
Duma FM loses 3pp market share to Gabz FM amid economic advertising pressure
Duma FM declined from 40% to 37% market share. Gabz FM gained 5pp through data-driven audience research attracting new advertisers, while Yarona FM refreshes programming to combat content fatigue.
𖠩
Revenue Rebound
Postal revenue rebounds +24.2% QoQ to BWP 24.75M from a Q3 low
Commercial postal operator revenue recovered strongly from BWP 19.75M to BWP 24.75M in Q4, led by Sprint Couriers (BWP 8.77M) and FedEx Express (BWP 7.37M), reversing Q3's 24.4% decline.
