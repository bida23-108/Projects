About BOCRA

The Botswana Communications Regulatory Authority (BOCRA) was established under the Communications Regulatory Authority (CRA) Act of 2012 and became operational on 1 April 2013 to regulate Botswana's communications sector, including telecommunications, ICTs, broadcasting, radio communications, and postal services. The Act replaced the Broadcasting and Telecommunications Acts and amended the Postal Services Act to create a single converged regulator.

BOCRA's mission is to regulate the communications sector to promote competition, innovation, consumer protection, and universal access, while its vision is to achieve a connected and digitally driven society. Its six strategic pillars are: competition, universal access and service, consumer protection, resource optimisation, talent management, and stakeholder engagement.

Licensing Framework

BOCRA is mandated by Section 6(h) of the CRA Act to process applications for and issue licences, permits, permissions, concessions and authorities for regulated sectors: telecommunications, internet, radio communications, broadcasting and postal.

Network Facilities Provider (NFP)

Licensees own, operate or provide physical infrastructure — fixed links, satellites, submarine cable, fibre/copper, towers, switches, base stations — for own use or for other licensed operators.

Services & Applications Provider (SAP)

Non-infrastructure-based providers offering speech, data, text, image and application services to end users over NFP infrastructure. Excludes broadcasting content services.

Content Services Provider (CSP)

Provides content material — audio, text, images, video — solely for broadcasting (TV and radio) and other information services including Subscription TV. State broadcasters are exempt.

Postal Licensing

Two categories: Designated Postal Operator (DPO) carrying universal service obligations, and Commercial Postal Operator (CPO) for value-added courier and postal services.

FAQs

Common questions about BOCRA services.

How do I apply for a licence?
Visit registration.bocra.org.bw to access the ASMS-WebCP portal. Select the appropriate licence category (NFP, SAP, CSP, or Postal), complete the application form, attach required supporting documents, and submit. BOCRA will acknowledge receipt and advise on next steps.
How do I file a complaint?
First, contact your service provider and allow them a reasonable opportunity to resolve the issue. If unsatisfied, submit a complaint to BOCRA via the online form at bocra.org.bw or in writing, including evidence of the issue and the provider's response.
What is Type Approval?
Type Approval is the process by which BOCRA certifies that communications equipment meets Botswana's technical standards before it can be used or sold in the country. Apply at typeapproval.bocra.org.bw.
Where can I view QoS results?
Quality of Service monitoring data and dashboards are available at dqos.bocra.org.bw. The portal shows coverage, speed test results, and service quality indicators for mobile operators.
How do I access BOCRA documents and guidelines?
All publications, acts, guidelines and reports are listed on the Documents & Legislation page. You can filter by category and download PDFs directly.
What is the Universal Access and Service Fund (UASF)?
Created in April 2014, the UASF finances provision of communication services in underserved and un-served areas of Botswana. It supports projects that bring connectivity to communities where commercial deployment would otherwise be uneconomical.
How do I verify if a business is a licensed operator?
Use the online Licence Verification tool at customerportal.bocra.org.bw or download the current BOCRA Licensee List from the Documents section.
